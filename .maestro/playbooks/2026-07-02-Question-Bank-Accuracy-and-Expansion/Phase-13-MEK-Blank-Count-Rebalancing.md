# Phase 13: MEK Blank-Count Rebalancing

Per Phase 06's audit, MEK (175 items) is 96.6% double-blank (169 of 175), with real exams mixing single-blank as the next-most-common pattern after double, and triple-blank as "a small minority but present every year." This is **not a retrofit of existing items** — Phase 06 was explicit that fixing this means writing *future* content differently, not editing the 175 existing questions (rewriting a sentence's blank structure would change what it's actually testing, unlike the mechanical fixes in other phases).

This phase writes **~15-20 new MEK questions, deliberately skewed toward single-blank and triple-blank**, to start correcting the bank's ratio going forward. This won't fully close the gap (that would take much more volume than is reasonable here) but establishes the pattern and meaningfully improves the mix.

**Write one question at a time, saving to the file before starting the next** — same pattern that's worked cleanly for Phases 10-12. Read minimally first, then write.
